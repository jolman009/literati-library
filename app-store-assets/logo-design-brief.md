# ShelfQuest Logo Design Brief

> **Brand**: ShelfQuest — Digital Library & Reading Companion
> **Domain**: https://shelfquest.org
> **Created**: 2026-03-31

---

## One-Line Concept

A book that's also a compass — the journey of reading as discovery.

## The Name Unpacked

"Shelf" = collection, home, personal library. "Quest" = journey, pursuit, leveling up. The logo should hold both halves — the comfort of your bookshelf and the forward motion of a quest.

---

## Brand Tone of Voice

Warm, encouraging, and gently playful. A knowledgeable friend who's excited about your reading life and respects your space. Sits between Duolingo's gamified energy and Kindle's quiet utility — more personality than a tool, less noise than a game.

## Brand Aesthetic

Modern material warmth with a scholarly edge. A modern library that feels like it was designed by someone who actually reads. The aesthetic rewards sustained attention — comfortable enough for a 2-hour reading session but polished enough to feel premium.

---

## Core Symbol Directions

### 1. The Open Book as Pathway (strongest direction)
An open book where the pages suggest a road, trail, or horizon line. Not literal — abstracted into clean geometric shapes. The spine becomes a starting point, the pages fan outward like a path opening up. It says "your reading takes you somewhere."

### 2. The Bookmark as Shield/Crest
A bookmark shape (tall, narrow, pointed base) used as a heraldic crest — nodding to the "quest" without being medieval. Inside, a minimal book or page icon. This plays well at small sizes (app icon, favicon) and connects to the achievement/gamification personality.

### 3. The Stacked Spine as Mountain
Book spines stacked vertically, silhouetted to suggest a mountain peak or summit. Reading as ascent. Clean, geometric, works in monochrome. Connects to the leveling-up, streak-building side of the brand.

---

## Tone & Feel

- **Warm but modern** — not corporate, not childish
- **Confident, not loud** — this is a reader's tool, it should feel intelligent and calm
- **Geometric with organic softness** — rounded corners (the whole UI uses 12-16px radii), no sharp edges, no harsh angles
- **Approachable premium** — think independent bookshop, not chain store. Think Moleskine, not Staples

---

## Color

- **Primary**: deep teal-blue `#0D74A0` — the logo should own this color
- **Accent possibility**: brighter `#24A8E0` for a highlight element or gradient
- Must work in single-color (monochrome), on dark backgrounds (white/light version), and at 16px favicon size
- No more than 2 colors in the mark itself

---

## Typography Direction (Wordmark)

- Clean sans-serif — Inter, DM Sans, or similar geometric humanist family
- Medium weight, not bold — confident without shouting
- Consider a subtle custom touch on one letter: maybe the Q in Quest has a tail that suggests a bookmark or page turn
- "Shelf" and "Quest" can be visually differentiated (weight, color, or spacing) but should read as one word

---

## What to Avoid

- Literal bookshelves (too clipart, too expected)
- Owls, graduation caps, lightbulbs (educational cliches)
- Overly ornate or serif-heavy marks (wrong era for this brand)
- Gaming aesthetics (swords, shields, pixel art) — the gamification is in the experience, not the brand mark
- Gradients that don't reduce to flat — the logo must work without gradient

---

## Size & Context Requirements

- **App icon** (512x512, 192x192, 96x96) — must be recognizable at 48px
- **Favicon** (16x16, 32x32) — the mark alone, no wordmark
- **Navigation rail header** — small, horizontal, paired with "ShelfQuest" text
- **Store listings** (Google Play, Microsoft Store, Chrome Web Store)
- **Splash screen / PWA launch**
- **Dark and light backgrounds**

---

## Reference Mood

The feeling of walking into a well-curated independent bookstore on a rainy afternoon — you know you're going to find something good, the space respects your time, and there's a quiet excitement about what you'll discover next.

---

## Closest Real-World Aesthetic Analogues

- **Notion's** cleanliness
- **Spotify's** dark-mode warmth
- **Apple Books'** respect for the content
